# Platform Tone Adaptation

Base voice is always [[brand-voice]]. These are per-platform adjustments.

## X / Twitter

- Most casual. Lowercase preferred. Short punchy lines
- Lead with the surprising number or contrarian take
- Okay to be slightly provocative — but always backed by data
- Use line breaks liberally. One thought per line
- No hashtags unless genuinely relevant (max 1-2)
- Threads: each tweet should standalone. Don't rely on context from tweet 1

## LinkedIn

- Slightly more professional but NOT corporate
- Still casual builder tone, just less internet-native slang
- Personal angle works well: "I noticed X in our data this week..."
- Longer form is fine (800-1500 words). Tell a story around the data
- Use bullet points and whitespace. LinkedIn walls of text don't work
- End with a question to drive comments

## Instagram

- Visual-first. The text supports the image/carousel, not the other way around
- Caption: conversational, like explaining to a friend. 150-300 words
- Carousel slides: bold claim on slide 1, data on slides 2-6, CTA on last slide
- Use our dashboard screenshots — they're visually distinctive
- Stories: behind-the-scenes of building, quick trend callouts

## TikTok

- Most raw and unfiltered. Screen recordings of the actual dashboard
- Script style: "okay so I just noticed something wild in the data..."
- 30-60 seconds. Get to the point fast
- Hook in first 2 seconds: state the surprising finding immediately
- No polish needed. Authenticity > production value
- End with "follow for more trend data" or similar soft CTA

## Blog (post-MVP)

- Most thorough. 1000-2500 words
- SEO-aware: target specific long-tail keywords
- Structure: hook → context → data → insight → implication → CTA
- Include embedded dashboard screenshots and score breakdowns
- Link to related trends and the Signal Eye explorer
- Write for [[audience/seo-teams]] primarily — they'll share it internally
